# Best Canny Alternatives in 2026

If you're searching for a **Canny alternative**, you're not alone. Canny has been a go-to feedback tool for years, but its tracked-user pricing model ‚Äî which starts at $99/mo and scales to $1,349/mo as your user base grows ‚Äî has pushed many SaaS teams to look elsewhere.

In 2026, the feedback tool landscape is more competitive than ever. New entrants with AI-native features, flat pricing, and modern UIs are giving Canny serious competition. Whether you're a bootstrapped startup or a scaling SaaS company, there's likely a better fit for your budget and workflow.

In this guide, we compare the **best Canny alternatives in 2026**, covering pricing, features, pros, cons, and who each tool is best for. Let's dive in.

## Why Teams Are Leaving Canny

Before we look at alternatives, it's worth understanding why so many teams are actively searching for a Canny replacement:

- **Tracked-user pricing is unpredictable.** Canny charges based on "tracked users" ‚Äî anyone who votes, comments, or submits feedback. As your product grows, so does your bill. Teams regularly report surprise invoices when a feature goes viral or they launch a public board.
- **The free plan was gutted.** Canny's free tier is extremely limited, making it hard to evaluate the product properly before committing.
- **No built-in AI.** Despite AI being table stakes in 2026, Canny still lacks native AI features like duplicate detection or auto-categorization.
- **Dated UI.** While functional, Canny's interface hasn't kept pace with newer, more polished competitors.

If any of these resonate, keep reading ‚Äî there are excellent alternatives available now.

**Verdict:** If you want the best value-for-money Canny alternative with genuine AI capabilities, Outcry is our top pick.

## 2. Featurebase

**Pricing:** Free plan available; paid plans from $29/seat/mo
**Website:** [featurebase.app](https://featurebase.app)

Featurebase has positioned itself as the modern Canny alternative and has gained significant traction. It offers feedback boards, roadmaps, changelogs, and a knowledge base in one platform.

### Pros

- Clean, modern UI that feels polished.
- Comprehensive feature set ‚Äî feedback, roadmap, changelog, and surveys all in one.
- Good integrations (Slack, Linear, Jira, Intercom).
- In-app widget for collecting feedback without leaving your product.
- AI-powered features including smart duplicate detection and auto-translation.

### Cons

- Some advanced features locked behind higher tiers.
- The free plan is limited and pushes you toward paid quickly.

### Who It's Best For

Mid-size teams that need an all-in-one feedback suite and don't mind per-seat pricing. If you're a solo founder or small team, the per-seat model may sting.

- Affordable starting price.
- Embeddable widget available.
- Changelog / announcements feature built in.
- Custom domain support.

### Cons

- **Limited AI capabilities.** No duplicate detection, no auto-categorization.

- Limited integrations compared to Featurebase or Canny.

### Who It's Best For

Small teams or indie hackers who want a simple, affordable feedback board without the complexity of larger tools. If you don't need AI features, Frill gets the job done.

## How to Choose the Right Canny Alternative

Choosing the best feedback tool depends on your team size, budget, and what you actually need. Here's a quick decision framework:

### Choose Outcry if...
You want the best balance of price, features, and AI. At $49/mo flat with AI duplicate detection, it's the most cost-effective option for growing SaaS teams. No tracked-user surprises, no per-seat scaling.

### Choose Featurebase if...
You need an all-in-one suite (feedback + roadmap + changelog + surveys) and your team is small enough that per-seat pricing stays reasonable.

### Choose Frill or Nolt if...
You want dead-simple feedback collection and don't need AI. Frill has more features; Nolt is simpler.

### Choose Upvoty or Sleekplan if...
Budget is your primary concern and you can live without AI features or a polished UI.

### Choose ProductBoard if...
You need full product management, not just feedback collection. ProductBoard makes sense when feedback is one piece of a larger product ops workflow.

### Choose Fider if...
You have engineering resources, want full data control, and don't mind self-hosting.

### Choose UserVoice if...
You're an enterprise with complex feedback workflows, revenue-weighted prioritization needs, and the budget to match.
